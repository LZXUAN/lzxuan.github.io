PSSHL

A WordPress blog created with love for the library of SMJK Hua Lian, Taiping

BACKGROUND

It all started with the evaluation of the library by the Ministry of Education of Malaysia. My school's library is competing for "The Best Secondary School's Library". One of the requirement is a blog. Since we (I'm a librarian) don't have one and I am the only one who know more about blog, I am given a task by our teacher, that is to build a blog. 

GOALS

  • Show the information of the secondary school
  • Display the information about the library
  • Update the latest news of the library

METHODOLOGY

OK. The Goals are set. It is the time to choose a suitable Content Manager System (CMS). Now, there are several CMSs that offer great services. Three good examples are WordPress, Joomla! and Drupal. Since, I am a beginner, I choosed WordPress. Below are the comparison and reasons I choosed WordPress.
CMS, checked. Now, hosting service. Since I don't have any money and never been provided, my target is a hosting company that provide free hosting service with at least 95% uptime and no ads. Although Byethost dosen't guarantee the server uptime, they still show their Server Status / Uptime Monitor. After checking, I found that Byethost meets my requirements and I'm surprised that it also provide auto script-installer which I can install WordPress easily. 

After installing WordPress on my Byethost account, I found that I need a free domain. So, I came up with Freenom. They provide 5 free top-level domains, including .tk, .ml, .ga, .cf and .gq. I choose .ml domain. So, the blog's URI is psshl.ml. Why "psshl"? Because it's the short-form for Library of SMJK Hua Lian in Malay language. 

After that, it's time to find a suitable theme for the blog. After searching and testing for a long time, I finally found the right theme, Customizr. The words I can use to describe Customizr are simply awesome and highly customizable. 

After setting up WordPress and Customizr theme, I installed a few plugins:

  • Akismet
  • Google Analytics Dashboard for WP
  • Jetpack by WordPress.com
  • SiteOrigin Widgets Bundle
  • W3 Total Cache
  • Wordfence Security

The final step is to update the blog. And now, it's all done. Below is the preview of the blog.

P.S. The main language used is Malay. This is to meet the requirement of the Ministry of Education of Malaysia. The other languages used included Chinese and English.
